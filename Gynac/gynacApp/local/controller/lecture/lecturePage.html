Methodology (TAS & TVS) and Tricks to Scan the Pelvis

Transabdominal and transvaginal scan: Basics, methodology, various signs, manoeuvres, tips and tricks of scanning the pelvis.

Ultrasound Settings and Technique

Information about the basic features and adjustments possible on the scanner and also how to modify them for the investigator’s best advantage for individual cases. Talk includes the discussion about the settings required for a good quality B mode (2D ultrasound) image and also for the colour, power and spectral Doppler with the minimum required physics, for the clinician.

Doppler in Gynecology

Types, Settings, Colour Scoring, 3D Doppler, advantages and utility in various case scenarios including malignancies, torsion etc.

Three Dimensional Ultrasound in Gynecology

Basics concepts, advantages, steps involved in 3D imaging, utility in various gynecological conditions using various display modes.

Three Dimensional Ultrasound in Gynecology

Enhanced Ultrasound Techniques (Gel Sonovaginography & Sonohysterography)

Gel Sonovaginography & Sonohysterography: Utility, methodology, advantages, limitations and application in various conditions.

Enhanced Ultrasound Techniques (Gel Sonovaginography & Sonohysterography)

Evaluation of Myometrium & Normal Myometrium

Evaluation of the uterine myometrium (primarily based on MUSA consensus statement): methodology, measurements, and qualitative assessment of normal and pathological lesions, junctional zone and normal variations.

Evaluation of Myometrium & Normal Myometrium

Fibroid (Leiomyoma) & Sarcoma

Ultrasound appearance & Doppler, fibroid mapping, 3D rendering, various cases of fibroid; large subserous, submucous and atypical cases. Red degeneration, fibroid embolization, diffuse uterine leiomyomatosis and disseminated peritoneal leiomyomatosis and the rare sarcoma.

Fibroid (Leiomyoma) & Sarcoma

Adenomyosis and Adenomyoma

Basic pathology and junctional zone involvement, variety of ultrasound appearance on 2D and 3D, subtle adenomyosis, posterior wall adenomyosis, adenomyosis secondary to obstruction, adenomyomas and differentiation from fibroids.

Congenital Uterine Anomalies

Embryopathogenesis and classification, approach to diagnosis, types of uterine anomalies, cervical & vaginal anomalies, hematocolpos and reporting of anomalies.

Evaluation of Endometrium & Normal Endometrium

Evaluation of the endometrium (primarily based on IETA consensus statement): methodology, measurements, qualitative assessment of normal and pathological lesions, Doppler evaluation, sonohysterogram and normal variations.

Endometrial Polyp & Hyperplasia

Endometrial polyps: Appearance and diagnosis with 2D, 3D, Doppler & SHG, atypical polyps, artefacts and malignant polyps. Endometrial Hyperplasia: Ultrasound appearance, Doppler & SHG, atypical hyperplasia and tamoxifen endometrium.

Endometrial Carcinoma

Ultrasound appearance on 2D, Doppler, 3D & 3D Doppler, ultrasound limitations, myometrial invasion, metastasis, atypical cases, malignant polyps and differential diagnosis of thickened endometrium.

Asherman’s Syndrome and sub-endometrial fibrosis: appearance and diagnosis on 2D & 3D Endometritis, intrauterine infection (including post-partum) and intra cavitatory fluid in uterus.

Ultrasound in Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome

Ultrasound criteria for diagnosis of polycystic ovaries. Stromal abundance and stromal vascularity for diagnosis & methods to establish stromal abundance. Correlation of hormonal & ultrasound features. Understanding and diagnosis of PCOS is logically explained in a simple, interesting and informative manner.

Cycle Assessment in Infertility and ART

Monitoring fertility treatment cycles, use of 3D & Doppler to diagnose low reserve, poor responders and polycystic ovarian syndrome & to decide the stimulation protocols, luteal phase assessment and correlation of Doppler findings of the ovary, follicle, uterus and endometrium with the hormonal status during natural and treatment cycles.

Tubal Assessment

Assessment of tubal patency with saline infusion salpingography using colour and pulse Doppler positive contrast (Hystero-contrast salpingography) HyCosy with volume ultrasound that has been proved to be one of the best modalities for tubal assessment after laparoscopy.

Oocyte Retrieval and Embryo Transfer

Description of both procedures that are done under ultrasound guidance is discussed in detail. This includes the pre-procedure preparation, anesthetic technique, minute details and practical tips of for both oocyte retrieval and embryo transfer techniques. Variations in the techniques and controversies are also discussed. The clips of the procedure on ultrasound screen illustrates the entire technique clear enough to practice.

Ultrasound Evaluation of Cervix

Normal cervix, evaluation of cervix on TVS along with tips to optimize imaging, evaluation of benign and malignant cervical pathology on 2D Doppler, 3D, 3D Doppler and gel sonovaginography.

Ultrasound Evaluation of Vagina

Evaluation of benign and malignant vaginal pathology on 2D Doppler, 3D, and gel sonovaginography. Brief note on vulval carcinoma.

Evaluation of Ovarian & Adnexal Pathology (based on IOTA Guidelines)

Evaluation of the ovarian & adnexal lesions (primarily based on IOTA recommendations): Measurements, terminology, Doppler assessment, morphological classification, description, determining origin of masses, simple rules, simple descriptors, and 3 stage approach to evaluating adnexal masses.

Ultrasound Evaluation of Normal Ovaries

Short talk on how to look and assess normal ovaries in various age groups.

Ovarian Masses & Spectrum of Ovarian Neoplasia - Part 1

Determining ovarian origin, physiological masses, endometriomas (including associated malignancy & decidualized cysts) and other non-neoplastic ovarian masses.

Ovarian Masses & Spectrum of Ovarian Neoplasia - Part 2

Spectrum of epithelial ovarian neoplasia (benign, borderline & malignant) – multiple cases with confirmed pathologies.

Ovarian Masses & Spectrum of Ovarian Neoplasia - Part 3

Spectrum of non-epithelial ovarian neoplasia (germ cell, stromal & metastatic tumors) – multiple cases of these rare tumors with confirmed pathologies.

Non Ovarian Adnexal Pathology – Part 1

Tubal pathology & PID: acute PID, pyosalpinx, tubo-ovarian masses, hydrosalpinx, tuberculosis and cases of fallopian tube carcinoma.

Non Ovarian Adnexal Pathology – Part 2

Non-tubal & non-ovarian adnexal pathology: Paraovarian cysts (including atypical ones), peritoneal inclusion cysts (along with tips for diagnosis) and other rare cases.

Practice - Evaluation of Ovarian & Adnexal Pathology (based on IOTA Guidelines)

This is an interesting practice session of evaluation of adnexal masses into benign or malignant after a quick revision of morphological classification, simple descriptors, simple rules and the 3 stage approach. There are multiple cases with sufficient intervening gap during the talk for the candidates to individually, logical evaluate and decide whether a mass is malignant or not.

Endometriosis Including Deep Infiltrating & Extra-Pelvic Endometriosis

Endometriomas revised, spectrum of deep infiltrating endometriosis – of bowel, bladder, uterosacral, ureters, vagina, & cervix, scar endometriosis and extra-pelvic thoracic & abdominal endometriosis.

Normal and Abnormal Early Pregnancy

Development and abnormalities of first 10-11 weeks of pregnancy. Embryonic development studied on ultrasound in B-mode & 3D, abnormalities that can be picked up so early in pregnancy and prognostic markers of pregnancy outcome in the first trimester.

Spectrum of Ectopic Pregnancies and PUL

Brief description of possibilities with intra uterine pregnancies, spectrum of ectopics – tubal, ovarian, interstitial, cornual, cervical, heterotropic, intramyometrial & scar ectopic and pregnancy of unknown location and their evaluation.

Retained Products of Conception

Variable ultrasound appearance on 2D and Doppler and retained placenta.

Gestational Trophoblastic Disease

Spectrum of GTD: molar pregnancy including partial mole and gestational trophoblastic neoplasia (Invasive mole, choriocarcinoma etc)

Torsion (Ovarian & Non-Ovarian)

Non-specific and the three specific classic features for diagnosis of torsion (including the whirlpool & the follicular ring sign) Non-ovarian torsion: of hydrosalpinx and paraovarian cyst.

Other Causes of Acute Pelvic Pain

Differential diagnosis of acute pelvic pain: An overview of causes (infection, ectopic, dysmenorrhea etc).

Uterine Vascular Malformations

Diagnosis of AV malformation, true A V malformations and differential diagnosis of vascular uterine lesions

Uterine Perforation & Uterovesical Fistula

Various cases of uterine perforation – At surgery, by IUCD and trophoblastic invasion. Also a case of utero-vesical fistula

Caesarean Scar Defect & Retroflexed Uterus

Caesarean scar defect: ultrasound appearance diagnosis, measurements and symptoms Retroflexed uterus: causes and effects.

Intrauterine Contraceptive Device

Types of IUCD and their appearance on 2D & 3D, displaced IUCD, IUCD perforation and associated infection.
